Wind Tunnel Physics

Angle of Attack: The angle between the wing's chord line and the oncoming airflow. Critical for understanding lift generation and stall behavior.

Lift: Force perpendicular to the airflow, generated by pressure differences above and below the wing surface.

Drag: Force parallel to the airflow, caused by air resistance and pressure differences.

Stall: Occurs when the angle of attack becomes too large, causing airflow separation and loss of lift.

Fan Speed: Controls the velocity of the airflow, affecting both lift and drag forces.
